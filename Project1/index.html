Welcome to the study

This study is designed to investigate the effectiveness of an online team-building experience. Please read all the instructions carefully.

You will complete a number of simple tasks together with other people, with whom you will be connected via the internet.

First, all participants in the study will select personal "avatars" and write short texts to introduce themselves.

Before beginning the tasks, you will spend 3 minutes with the other people. During this time, you will be able to read and react to each-other's brief introductions. During this 3 minutes, you will be able to get to know your potential team members better.
Later on, you will be on a team with another person to work on some tasks

Group Introduction

Soon after an internet connection with the other participants is established, you should be able to see all the other participants’ descriptions. Several other participants will be connected with you through the internet and will concurrently work on the same task. You will ‘meet’ and interact with the other participants through an established page. During the interaction, you will be able to get to know your potential team members better.

Even though your interaction is minimal, please try to form an impression of the people in the group. Try to imagine them in real life - how they might look or sound, what kind of people they are, how you would get along with them.

The task will last 3 minutes and is very important to the study, so please be attentive, without switching pages, or doing unrelated tasks. Questions about the other people might follow.

You can read and react to the short descriptions the other participants wrote about themselves by clicking a "like" button, similar to the "like" button on Facebook ("favorite" on Twitter, ♥ in Tumblr and Instagram, etc.).

You can also make comments about other people's descriptions, by clicking the message icon near the other person’s name.

Please make sure to make at least two comments and send them to the other participants.

If these instructions are clear, you can proceed to log in.

What next?


Now, you will team up with one of the people you have been interacting with, and will be solving a set of challenging logical problems as a team. You will be randomly assigned to one of the following tasks:
  • spatial reasoning task,
  • visual/perceptual task,
  • logical reasoning task,
  • mathematical problems task.

This problem solving activity is expected to foster engagement, improve communication and encourage cooperation between teammates.

Some of you will be able to choose one person out of the three people as your teammate. Some of you will be randomly assigned a teammate to work with.

The top two teams that complete the most problems will each win $30 Amazon gift certificates.



Choosing your partner

You are randomly assigned to work on the mathematical problems with your partner.

The mathematical problems are pulled from the MCAT (Medical College Admission Test) math test pool.
