What next?


Now, you will team up with one of the people you have been interacting with,
and will be solving a set of challenging logical problems as a team. You will
be randomly assigned to one of the following tasks:
  • spatial reasoning task,
  • visual/perceptual task,
  • logical reasoning task,
  • mathematical problems task.

This problem solving activity is expected to foster engagement, improve communication
and encourage cooperation between teammates.

Some of you will be able to choose one person out of the three people as your teammate.
Some of you will be randomly assigned a teammate to work with.

The top two teams that complete the most problems will each win
$30 Amazon gift certificates.

Choosing your partner

You are randomly assigned to work on the mathematical problems with your partner. Mathematical problems are
pulled out from MCAT (Medical college admission test) math test pool.You are assigned to the condition in which
you can choose a partner to solve the math problems with. Please click on the name of your preferred partner.

Thank you very much for your participation in this study.

The primary purpose of this study is to examine how a threat to one's sense of "honor" may influence may
influence goal-directed behaviors. We created the "Online team-building experience" as a cover story to
make the tasks we were asking you to do seem legitimate and realistic. In fact, all the other participants
you interacted with were not real but computerized hypothetical participants,and all the interactive elements
were simulated through predefined computer scripts. We were interested in how participants react to an
accusation of lying.

Some participants received the feedback from one other participant who accused them of lying. Others
received the negative or neutral feedback instead of being accused. In fact, the feedback was pre-scripted
manipulation,not the actual response from other participants.

We will examine the relation of the value scales you completed at the beginning, the emotion scales, and your
decisions of the teammate for the mathematical problems. The characteristics of potential teammates were
designed to measure your behavioral responses to accusation or other feedback and we expected that if you
were accused, you would be less likely to choose the best candidate compared to other conditions.

We are truly sorry that we had to create a study that involved misleading information, but we hope you
understand that to avoid raising suspicion, it was necessary to use a misleading cover story. We also want
to reassure you that all your responses will remain completely confidential. No identifying data will be
maintained with your data.



If we told our participants the true purpose of the study, people would probably
not respond as they would in real life. Please do not tell other students the true
purpose of the study. If participants know, for example, that the other participants
do not exist, we won't get useful results. If someone asks you what the studies are
about, you might tell them that it is online teambuilding activities involving completing
scales and several forms of interactions with other participants.

We hope that this research will contribute to our understanding of how people emotionally
and behaviorally respond to threats to their honor.
